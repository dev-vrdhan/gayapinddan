Mela Overview & Spiritual Importance

The 15 days when the gates of heaven open, and ancestor spirits visit Earth seeking peaceful release.

The Pitr Paksha Mela is one of the largest and oldest religious gatherings in India, held annually in the sacred city of Gaya, Bihar. Spanning 15 days during the Hindu month of Ashwin (September-October), this festival is dedicated entirely to ancestral remembrance and the performance of Shraddha rites.

According to ancient texts, during Pitru Paksha, the Sun enters the zodiac sign of Virgo. This astral alignment allows the souls of our ancestors (Pitrus) to leave their heavenly/interstellar realms and descend to Earth, seeking food and water offerings from their living descendants. Performing Pind Daan during this specific window yields infinite merits, immediately satisfying the souls and ending any residual ancestral debt (Pitra Rin).

Standard 17-Day Daily Schedule

For pilgrims wishing to perform the full traditional 17-day (17-Vedi) pilgrimage, the daily route is structured below.

Day 1

Punpun River Rites

Punpun River (Gaya Entry)

First ritual purification performed on the way to Gaya. Pilgrims pay respect at the banks of Punpun before entering the main city bounds.

Day 2

Falgu River Tarpan

Devghat, Falgu River

Initial water tarpan offerings to ancestors using sand pinds on the banks of the sacred cursed river Falgu.

Day 3

Vishnupad Footprints

Vishnupad Temple Sanctum

Offering of main rice pinds directly on the basalt stone footprints of Lord Vishnu to invoke divine sanction.

Day 4 - 8

Hills Pilgrimage

Pretshila & Ramshila Hills

Performing rites on the peaks of Pretshila and Ramshila to pacify ancestors who passed away under sudden or unnatural conditions.

Day 9 - 14

Pond & Kund Offerings

Brahma Kund, Uttarka Manas

Immersions and customized Shraddha offerings at various historic ponds surrounding the central Gaya temple circle.

Day 15 - 17

Akshay Vat Conclusion

Akshay Vat Tree Roots

Final prayers, tying of sacred threads, Gau Daan, and Brahman Bhojan to complete the ancestral journey and secure eternal blessings.

Travel & Logistics Information

Nearby Railway Station

Gaya Junction (GAYA) is the primary railway head, located within the city limits (approx 4 km from Vishnupad Temple). It is well-connected to major cities like Delhi, Kolkata, Patna, Mumbai, and Varanasi with frequent express trains.

Nearest Airport

Gaya International Airport (GAY) is located at Bodhgaya, about 10 km from the temple. It handles flights from domestic nodes like Kolkata and Delhi. **Patna Airport (PAT)** is 115 km away, offering broader nationwide connectivity.

Accommodation options

Gaya offers options ranging from basic Dharamshalas maintained by pandas to premium tourist hotels in Bodhgaya. Government tourist lodges (Bihar Tourism) are also setup. Booking months in advance is essential during Mela season.

Mela Weather: September/October in Gaya has warm and humid days (28°C - 33°C) with cooler nights (21°C - 24°C). Periodic post-monsoon showers occur. Dress in light cottons.

Do's and Don'ts for Pilgrims

Follow these code-of-conduct guidelines to ensure a spiritually pure and safe experience during your stay.

Do's

  • Book a verified panda with ancestral record books (Khata) to authenticate lineage records.
  • Wear traditional white cotton clothes (Dhoti/Kurta for men, Saree/Suits for women) during rituals.
  • Keep a list of departed ancestral names, dates of passing (tithi), and your family Gotra ready.
  • Drink only bottled water and consume simple satvik vegetarian meals during the ritual period.
  • Take a holy bath in the Falgu River or Devghat before performing any pind offerings.

Don'ts

  • Do not consume alcohol, meat, onion, garlic, or tobacco during your entire stay in Gaya.
  • Avoid wearing dark black clothing or leather items (belts, wallets, shoes) inside temples.
  • Do not engage in arguments or aggressive bargaining with local service providers or pandas.
  • Do not throw plastic, garbage, or synthetic items into the Falgu river beds or sacred sites.
  • Do not pay random agents or brokers on roads; deal directly with certified Panda Ji groups.

Checklist & Packing List
